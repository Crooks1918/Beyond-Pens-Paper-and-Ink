# Beyond Pens, Paper, and Ink: Evaluating Council Spending

This project examines Rochdale Borough Council’s expenditure between July 2024 and June 2025, focusing on efficiency, consistency, and transparency in public spending. Local councils, including Rochdale, face high demand for services and ongoing financial pressures, making even small, recurring costs like office supplies important indicators of departmental behaviour and cost-efficiency.

## Project Purpose

The project aims to answer: “How much does a council spend on stationery?” while also exploring broader themes of financial accountability.

Key steps include:

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Identify trends in expenditure over time, department spending, and revenue generation.

Anomaly Detection: Highlight departments with unusually high anomalous transactions, signaling inefficiencies or irregularities.

Final Analysis: Examine significant budgets and transactions, departmental expenditure trends, and routine costs as a case study.

By combining quantitative analysis with context, this project provides insights into spending patterns, highlights potential concerns, and contributes to transparent and responsible public finance management.
